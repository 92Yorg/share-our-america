Share Our America is a growing community that brings people together across difference — to foster greater empathy, understanding, respect and trust.

We envision an America where difference strengthens our communities, not divides them.

Learn about the impact from our inaugural pilot in Nebraska

Americans are growing more divided.

We feel it every day in our country, our communities, and even our homes. As polarization rises, Americans increasingly dislike, distrust and dehumanize one another.

However, reality is very different from our perception; Democrats and Republicans believe that people from the other party dislike them 2x more than they actually do. This has dire consequences for our country and exacerbates the divide that Americans see and feel on a daily basis. The majority of Americans say that talking about politics with someone they disagree with is stressful and frustrating. As a result, Americans either disengage with each other for fear of conflict, or engage with heightened tension, leading to distrust, antagonism, and even hatred.

And yet, we are hopeful.

Despite everything, the vast majority of Americans (86%) think we can improve our trust in one another. Share Our America is born from this optimism and hope, and is for every American who believes change in our country is possible.

We believe in a country where difference strengthens our communities, not divides them.

Share Our America is a growing community of people and organizations who believe that change can happen — and that begins on a one-to-one basis, person by person, community by community. By bringing Americans together as humans — to connect with one other, to listen — we can start to understand each other's different perspectives, gain empathy, respect and trust.

If each of us commits to sharing our America, and then our neighbors commit, and then our fellow Americans in the communities around us commit, and on and on, we can start to spread trust and respect and hope through our country that influences our institutions, schools and governments.

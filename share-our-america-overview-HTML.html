Share Our America is a domestic cultural exchange program bringing Americans of different backgrounds closer together. With an initial focus on adults 60+, participants will travel to parts of the U.S. they haven’t been exposed to and explore life through a local perspective, building towards greater empathy and understanding.

Political, ideological, and geographic polarization are on the rise in America. Over the past 25 years, Americans have moved further apart on issues. Today, partisanship is the dividing factor on policy issues ‐ surpassing age, race / ethnicity, education, gender, and religion.

As polarization rises, Americans increasingly dislike, distrust, and dehumanize one another, with 42% of partisans seeing the “other side” as evil and >50% of Americans having few or no friends from the opposing political party. This has dire consequences for our country.

However, we are ready for change: 86% of Americans think we can improve our trust in one another and many highlight the importance of engagement with local communities to do so. Drawing on research-backed strategies to bridge divides, Share Our America is a unique domestic cultural exchange program, bringing Americans of different backgrounds closer together.

Participants will travel to Chadron, NE, a rural community in the picturesque pine ridge of Western Nebraska. During the 4 day, 3 night experience, participants will stay with local hosts and explore life through the community’s perspective. We will spend a day shadowing a farmer or rancher, socialize with the community, do a service activity, and deepen our understanding of different viewpoints through facilitated dialogues.

Bridging religious and political divides across geographies

Chadron, Nebraska

May 19-22, 2022

Share Our America’s inaugural pilot was jointly run by the Belfer Center for Innovation & Social Impact at 92NY and the Episcopal Diocese of Nebraska. A small group of participants from rural Nebraska met over four days with urban and suburban areas across the United States, including California, New Jersey, New York and Florida.

Participants worked through facilitated conversations about their differences, social activities, shared meals, a homestay, a visit to a local ranch, and all the moments in between.

Supported by Essential Partners, a global leader in dialogue and facilitation, 92NY and the Episcopal Diocese facilitated group conversations that encouraged participants to share personal stories and listen without judgment. The group bonded over discussions of family, what Main Street means to them, and how they want to continue to have more meaningful conversations across differences in their towns and communities.

After the gathering

Participants reported increased empathy and understanding of others — and feeling more understood themselves.

  • 100% reported better understanding the feelings and beliefs of people whose opinions and backgrounds differ from theirs.
  • 91% reported feeling more understood by people whose opinions and backgrounds differ from theirs.
  • 100% of participants planned to share what they’ve learned with friends and family.

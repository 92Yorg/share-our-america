Impact

Share Our America’s inaugural pilot, jointly run by the Belfer Center for Innovation & Social Impact at 92NY and the Episcopal Diocese of Nebraska, brought together participants representing a spectrum of political perspectives, religious values and traditions, and lived experiences. Over four days, participants got to know each other through facilitated conversations about their differences, social activities, shared meals, a homestay, a visit to a local ranch, and all the moments in between.

“Just listening was incredibly valuable. Hearing from the fellow participants and reflecting on my own life, ideas and values. Sitting face to face, eye to eye, with a host whose ideas regarding abortion were the antithesis of mine; hearing her deep conviction.

Lilian, New York, NY

What we’re hearing

    • 100% of participants reported better understanding the feelings and beliefs of people whose opinions and backgrounds differ from theirs
    • 100% of participants reported developing meaningful relationships through the experience, and planned to keep in touch with people they met who are different from them
    • 100% of participants planned to share what they've learned with friends and family

  • 82% of participants reported having greater respect for people whose opinions, beliefs, and backgrounds differ from theirs
  • 82% of participants reported that their ability to communicate with people who are different from them has improved, as well as their ability to listen to opinions and beliefs far removed from their own without judgment
